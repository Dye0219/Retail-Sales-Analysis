🎯Objectives:

• Organized and validated sales data in Microsoft Excel, modeled a STAR schema with Power Query, and built interactive dashboards in Microsoft Power BI with layouts designed in Canva.

• Created key performance metrics such as average order value, average selling price, sales trends, and YoY growth to support data-driven planning and operational efficiency.

• Analyzed transactional sales data to identify revenue drivers, customer purchasing behavior, and discount impact to optimize pricing strategies and profit margins.

📊Key Insights:

• Despite 33% of the discount data being missing, meaningful insights can still be drawn regarding the impact of discounts on revenue.

• Revenue declined by 3.89% in 2023 compared to 2022, but rebounded with a 6.95% increase in 2024, indicating a recovery phase.

• Discounts appear to encourage customers to purchase more items and in greater quantities, contributing to revenue growth. However, customers generally tend to buy only one unit per item across all years.

• High-priced items in categories of butchers, electric household essentials, beverages, and furniture generates revenue throughout the year, indicating strong market demand for premium-priced goods. In contrast, low-priced items contribute less to revenue and show weaker market performance.

• Seasonal trends show that January consistently records peak revenue, while February experiences the lowest performance each year.

• In terms of purchasing channels, cash payments and physical store transactions generate higher revenue, suggesting that customers still prefer traditional shopping methods over digital alternatives.

• A notable example is Customer 5, who purchased 1,111 discounted items over the year, emphasizing the effectiveness of discounts in driving volume sales.

• Interestingly, although overall revenue declined in 2022, customers increased their engagement with discounts. However, these purchases were primarily focused on lower-priced items, which limited their impact on overall revenue growth.

• Overall, customers show a clear preference for high-value products, regardless of whether discounts are applied. On average, each customer purchases 5–6 items per year.
